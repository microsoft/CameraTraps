This demo runs a detector model that was trained to find animals in camera trap images.The code used to train this model is available on GitHub, and most of the data used to train this model is available at our open data repository, lila.science.

This is just a prototype, but we believe we can help conservation biologists who are faced with the challenge of eliminating vast numbers of empty images from their camera trap data sets. We are also looking for collaborators are we move toward species identification models. If you are interested in working with us on either of these problems, if you have data you’d like to contribute, or if you are an application developer interested in building other systems on top of this model, please contact us at cameratraps@microsoft.com.

The API runs on the AI for Earth API backend, which makes it easy for anyone working on machine learning for sustainability and environmental science to turn their models into APIs and Web-based applications. If you’re working in this space and you need cloud computing resources to bring your work to life, check out our AI for Earth grants program.
